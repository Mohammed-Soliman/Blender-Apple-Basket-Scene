# Blender-Apple-Basket-Scene

This repository contains the final project for the **Blender for Beginners** course, showcasing a photorealistic 3D still life scene featuring apples, a rustic basket, a knife, and a cloth backdrop.

**Course:** Blender for Beginners  
**Platform:** CG Boost  

---

## 🚀 Project Overview

This project demonstrates a complete 3D art pipeline workflow for beginners using Blender. It transitions from basic block-outs to high-fidelity asset creation across several interconnected disciplines:

- **3D Modeling & Sculpting**: Creating organic apple shapes, a detailed woven basket, and a sharp kitchen knife.
- **Shading & Texturing**: Applying realistic PBR textures, setting up organic procedural shaders for apple skins, and mapping burlap fabrics.
- **Lighting & Composition**: Utilizing standard photographic lighting principles within the Cycles engine to create a cinematic, dramatic mood.

---

## 📘 Topics Covered

### 1️⃣ 3D Asset Modeling
- **Organic Modeling**: Sculpting high-resolution apples with realistic imperfections, indents, stems, and leaves.
- **Hard Surface Modeling**: Drafting the knife blade, handle, and structural elements with clean topology and proper beveling.
- **Deformation & Placement**: Utilizing basic physics or manual orientation to realistically settle the apples inside the basket.

### 2️⃣ Materials & Shading
- **Procedural Shading**: Generating dynamic variations in color, roughness, and subsurface scattering for realistic fruit skin.
- **PBR Texture Mapping**: Applying and scaling seamless image textures for the wooden tabletop and burlap cloth.
- **UV Unwrapping**: Unwrapping complex meshes to ensure zero texture stretching or distortion across surfaces.

### 3️⃣ Lighting & Final Rendering
- **Cycles Render Engine**: Configuring path tracing settings for photorealistic global illumination and glass/water refractions.
- **Studio Lighting Setup**: Setting up key, fill, and rim lights to accentuate the micro-textures of the fruit and fabric.
- **Camera Composition**: Tuning focal length, framing, and depth of field (DoF) to emphasize the primary focal points.

---

## 🎨 Core Elements Demonstrated

- **The Apples**: Realistic variety in color gradients, specular roughness, and subtle surface bumps.
- **The Basket**: Interlocking structural geometry simulating a traditional woven texture.
- **The Fabric**: High-resolution burlap cloth showcasing detailed displacement and fine-thread overlays.
- **The Knife & Table**: Complementary elements adding a narrative touch and sharp reflections to the scene composition.

---

## 💻 Software & Engine Settings

- **Software**: Blender (v3.x / v4.x preferred)
- **Render Engine**: Cycles (Feature Set: Supported, Device: GPU Compute)
- **Post-Processing**: Blender Compositor (Denoising, Color Management, Filmic/AgX look)

---

## 🔍 Workflow Steps Included

- **Phase 1: Scene Blocking**: Establishing initial camera scale, focal lengths, and broad silhouette placements.
- **Phase 2: High-Poly Modeling**: Adding fine details, edge loops, and sculpting organic variations onto the meshes.
- **Phase 3: Material Assignment**: Building node trees for complex material behaviors like fruit translucency and fabric bump mapping.
- **Phase 4: Composition & Final Render**: Tweaking lighting orientations, adjusting sample counts, and rendering out the final pass.
